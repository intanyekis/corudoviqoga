Early Life

Born Daystar Shemuel Shua Peterson on July 27, 1992, in Brampton, Ontario, Canada, Tory Lanez’s journey to success was marked by adversity and determination. Raised in a tumultuous environment following his mother’s passing, Lanez found solace in music, using it as a means to express himself and navigate life’s challenges. Inspired by the likes of Notorious B.I.G., Lanez honed his craft and adopted the moniker “Tory Lanez,” embarking on a path that would ultimately lead him to stardom.

Tory Lanez Career

Lanez’s rise to prominence began with the release of his mixtape “T.L 2 T.O” in 2009, followed by a series of successful projects that showcased his raw talent and innovative sound. His signing with Sean Kingston’s record label, Time is Money Entertainment, marked a pivotal moment in his career, leading to the release of several mixtapes and ultimately, his debut studio album, “I Told You,” in 2016. The album’s commercial success, coupled with hit singles like “Say It” and “Luv,” solidified Lanez’s position as a rising star in the music industry.
