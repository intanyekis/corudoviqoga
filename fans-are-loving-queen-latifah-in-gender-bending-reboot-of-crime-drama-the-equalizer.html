Fans Are Loving Queen Latifah In Gender-Bending Reboot Of Crime Drama 'The Equalizer'

Publish date: 2024-05-25

Queen Latifah is the star of the latest gender-bending reboot of crime drama The Equalizer.

The vigilante character was originally portrayed by British actor Edward Woodward in a 1980s series airing on NBC. Denzel Washington then played the role in the 2014 movie of the same name and its 2018 sequel.

In the brand-new series premiered on CBS on February 7 after the Super Bowl, Robert “Bob” McCall turns into Robyn McCall, played by Latifah.

Fans Are Onboard With Queen Latifah’s Gender-Bending Version Of ‘The Equalizer’

The first reactions to Latifah’s turn as McCall are definitely positive, praising the show’s take on the superhero genre.

“Whelp. #TheEqualizer is a hit show. I just hope @IAMQUEENLATIFAH is prepared to be kicking ass for a decade!!!! Congrats to everyone involved!” actress Tracie Thoms wrote on Twitter.

“[The] Equalizer is the most fun I’ve seen on TV in a long time. @IAMQUEENLATIFAH is the perfect superhero, going to bat for the voiceless, and doing it with style,” a fan commented.

“Queen Latifah is so amazing in the Equalizer!! Perfect casting! So excited for this new series!” was another comment.

The Equalizer Cast Features ‘Sex And The City’ Star

The series also stars Sex and the City alum Chris Noth in the role of an ex-CIA director, as well as Adam Goldberg in the role of a hacker and Liza Lapira as Melody, a former Air Force sniper turned bar owner and friend of Robyn. McCall’s daughter Delilah is played by Laya DeLeon Hayes.

Queen Latifah reacted to the outpouring of support by tweeting her very own Equalizer joke following the season premiere.

“I think I speak for everyone when I say,” she wrote, captioning a GIF of herself saying “damn”.

The Equalizer airs on CBS on Sundays
