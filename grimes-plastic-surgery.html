Grimes underwent plastic surgery? Claire Elise Boucher, a Canadian singer, claims she underwent an experimental eye procedure to block out blue light.

Grimes made an unusual statement about her workout routine when she announced her campaign with Adidas and Stella McCartney in July 2019.

“My training is a 360-degree approach,” she explained at the time. “I do a 1-2 hour sword fighting session with my trainer, James Lew, in the afternoons.” We go over the fundamentals of obliques, core stabilisers, and triceps exercises, as well as a few tricks. To unwind, I spend 30-45 minutes on an inclined hike at 4-4.5 miles per hour, which is arguably the most efficient workout.”

Fans began to wonder if the Art Angels artist was trolling them when she went on to say that she replaced part of her eyeball with plastic to treat seasonal depression.

“I’ve also eliminated all blue light from my vision through an experimental surgery that removes the top film of my eyeball and replaces it with an orange ultra-flex polymer that my friend and I created in the lab this past winter to treat seasonal depression,” she added.

Fans speculated in 2018 that the mother of two — who shares son X A-Xii and daughter Exa Dark Siderael Musk with Tesla CEO Elon Musk — had fillers.

“It does appear that she has had fillers…?” “I’ve been thinking the same, she looks beautiful with or without,” one Reddit user wrote, while another agreed, “I’ve been thinking the same, she looks beautiful with or without.” Meanwhile, a third opined, “This looks like different lighting, makeup, and Photoshop to me, but OK.”

Grimes later admitted to getting lip fillers, according to reports.

Although the songwriter rarely discusses her appearance, she recently shut down haters who criticized her appearance. Grimes wrote on Instagram in April 2020, “People make fun of my big nose and weird face, but I think it’s cool to be strange.” It took me a long time to figure out how to make makeup look good because standard tricks don’t work. But I’m grateful for my unusual design.”

Career

She released Geidi Primes, her debut studio album, in January 2010.

Her concept album was inspired by Grimes and the influential science fiction book Dune by Frank Herbert.

Grimes’ second album, “Halfaxa,” was released in October of the same year.

She then started promoting her music outside of Montreal and served as the support act for Lykke Li’s North American tour.

After joining the 4AD record label, she released her debut album, Visions, in January 2012.

The album, which was fully recorded using Apple’s GarageBand software during three drug-filled weeks, was well-received by music critics and featured the top hits “Genesis” and “Oblivion.”

Grimes released “Art Angels,” her critically acclaimed fourth studio album, in November 2015.
Grimes topped the Billboard Top Alternative Albums chart with Art Angels.

She released “Miss Anthropocene,” her most recent album, on the 4AD record label in February 2020.

The musician joined Columbia Records in March 2021.
